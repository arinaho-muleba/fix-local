Community comes together to fix potholes in Mahikeng

In Mahikeng, people are stepping up to fix the pothole problem, showing how working together can make local roads better.

What was the problem?

Potholes are a big issue in South Africa, making it tough to keep roads safe. In Mahikeng, many streets are full of dangerous potholes. The local government has struggled to fix the roads, which makes residents unhappy.

How did they solve it?

People in Mahikeng want to improve their community, so they are fixing the potholes themselves. Abednigo Shebi from Phatsima Village is one person who uses broken bricks to patch potholes. Young people without jobs are helping with this effort. They want to help the community and earn some money.

The Vala Zonke project is getting more support from the provincial government and local councils. This project aims to fix potholes and make roads safer, with help from community members who care about Mahikeng.

How did they do it?

  • People like Abednigo Shebi use things like broken bricks and cement to fill potholes.
  • Young people in the community are working hard to fix roads and earn money for their families.
  • The Vala Zonke project brings the government and local people together to fix the problem.
